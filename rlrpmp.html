Richmond Long Range Property Management Plan Parcels

Parcels Of Land Now Owned By The City of Richmond

Map authored by Matthew Bacinskas

Additional information about the data and map goes here.

This map shows parcels of land that are now owned by the City of Richmond that were formerly owned by the Successor Agency to the Redevelopment Agency. All the information for the parcels was obtained from Richmond's Long Range Property Management Plan document. Parcel polygon data was downloaded from the Contra Costa County Mapping Information Center. Storefront data was downloaded from Dillon Mahmoudi's GitHub page. Dillon Mahmoudi was part of the Storefront Index project with Joe Cortright at City Observatory and graciously made the data available. The Storefront Index mapped businesses that are within 100m of another business. The storefronts included in this map from the Storefront Index are located within a 400m (1/4 mile) buffer distance of the parcels now owned by the city. A 400m buffer distance was used since it is a relatively easy distance to walk for most people. Storefront sites will become visible as you zoom into the map and the Parcel Location layer dissolves, storefronts will appear as purple markers. Click the parcel polygons to reveal its Assesor's Parcel Number(APN) and planned use.
